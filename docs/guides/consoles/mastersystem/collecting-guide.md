# Sega Master System Collecting Guide

## Introduction

The Sega Master System occupies a unique position in retro gaming collecting. While overshadowed by the NES in North America and Japan, it achieved remarkable success in Europe, Brazil, and Australia, creating distinct regional collecting scenes. For collectors, the SMS offers affordable entry points, interesting regional variations, and one of the most accessible complete set goals in retro gaming.

### Why Collect for Master System?

**Accessibility:**

- Smaller library than NES (easier complete set)
- Generally lower prices than equivalent NES titles
- Hardware readily available and reliable
- Strong community support

**Quality Library:**

- Several genuine classics (Phantasy Star, Wonder Boy III)
- Excellent arcade ports for the era
- Underrated platformers and action games
- FM sound enhances many Japanese releases

**Unique Appeal:**

- Regional variations add depth
- Brazilian market offers exclusives
- FM sound collecting subset
- 3D glasses gaming experience
- Strong European collector community

**Historical Significance:**

- Sega's foundation for later success
- Technically superior to NES
- Important stepping stone to Genesis era

## Getting Started

### Entry-Level Budget: $100-$250

For newcomers, focus on:

1. **Console (SMS original preferred)** - ($60-$120) for card slot
2. **Second controller** - ($10-$20)
3. **AV cables** - ($10-$15)
4. **5-8 starter games** - Affordable classics

**Recommended Starter Set:**

- Sonic the Hedgehog ($12-$25)
- Alex Kidd in Miracle World ($15-$30)
- Space Harrier ($10-$20)
- Shinobi ($15-$30)
- Fantasy Zone ($15-$30)
- OutRun ($10-$22)
- Hang-On/Safari Hunt (pack-in) ($5-$12)

**Total Investment:** ~$150-$280 for a solid foundation

### Which Console to Choose?

**Original Master System (SMS):**
✅ Card slot for Sega Card games
✅ Better build quality
✅ More complete experience
✅ Preferred by collectors
❌ Larger, heavier
❌ More expensive

**Master System II:**
✅ More affordable
✅ Built-in game (usually)
✅ Compact design
❌ No card slot
❌ Some exclusives unplayable

### Building Your Collection

**Phase 1: Core Classics ($150-$400 total)**
Essential games that define the platform.

**Phase 2: Deep Cuts ($400-$1,000)**
Quality titles beyond the obvious choices.

**Phase 3: Regional & Rare ($1,000-$3,000+)**
European exclusives, Japanese imports, card games.

## Regional Collecting Strategies

### North American Focus

**Advantages:**

- Smallest official library (~318 games)
- Most achievable complete set
- Generally lower prices
- Straightforward collecting

**Challenges:**

- No FM sound
- Missing European exclusives
- Fewer variant collecting options

**Complete Set Strategy:**

1. Start with common titles ($3-$15 range)
2. Work through mid-tier games
3. Save Phantasy Star and rare titles for last
4. Estimated cost: $3,000-$6,000 (loose)

### European (PAL) Focus

**Advantages:**

- Largest library with exclusives
- Active collector community
- Some games only released here
- RGB SCART video output

**Challenges:**

- 50Hz timing on some games
- Higher prices for exclusives
- PAL-specific compatibility

**Key Exclusives to Target:**

- Power Strike II (must-have)
- Land of Illusion
- Deep Duck Trouble
- Streets of Rage
- Sonic Chaos/Spinball

### Japanese Focus

**Advantages:**

- FM sound enhances many games
- Mark III collecting option
- Card format variety
- Different box art/packaging

**Challenges:**

- Import costs and shipping
- Language barrier (minimal for action games)
- Different cartridge form factor

**FM Sound Priority Games:**

- Phantasy Star (dramatically enhanced)
- Ys: The Vanished Omens
- Wonder Boy III
- Shinobi
- Space Harrier

### Brazilian (Tec Toy) Focus

**Advantages:**

- Unique exclusive titles
- Still in production (some)
- Interesting cultural adaptations
- Growing collector interest

**Challenges:**

- Shipping from Brazil
- Condition concerns (humidity)
- Authenticity verification

## Storage and Preservation

### Cartridge Care

**Cleaning:**

1. Use isopropyl alcohol (90%+)
2. Clean pins with cotton swab
3. Work in one direction
4. Allow complete drying

**Storage:**

- Store vertically
- Keep in protective cases or boxes
- Avoid humidity and temperature extremes
- Consider silica gel packets for humid climates

### Card Game Storage

Sega Cards are fragile:

- Store in protective sleeves
- Avoid bending or pressure
- Keep away from magnetic fields
- Handle by edges only

### Console Preservation

**Common Issues:**

- Cartridge slot wear
- Capacitor aging (audio issues)
- Video output degradation
- Power switch wear

**Maintenance:**

- Clean cartridge slot periodically
- Consider recapping older units
- Test regularly
- Store in climate-controlled space

## FM Sound Collecting

### Why FM Sound Matters

The Yamaha YM2413 FM sound chip dramatically enhances games:

- Richer, fuller music
- Additional audio channels
- Closer to arcade experience
- Significant improvement on many titles

### How to Access FM Sound

**Option 1: Japanese Hardware**

- Purchase Japanese SMS (built-in FM)
- Or Mark III with FM Sound Unit
- Japanese carts play on any SMS

**Option 2: Modification**

- FM sound board can be added
- Professional installation recommended
- Enables FM on all SMS games that support it
