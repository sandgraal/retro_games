# Collecting RPGs: The Complete Guide

_Last updated: January 2025_

## Introduction

Role-playing games represent the crown jewels of many retro game collections. From the earliest dungeon crawlers to the cinematic experiences of the PlayStation era, RPGs offer some of the deepest, most memorable gaming experiencesâ€”and some of the most valuable collectibles.

**Why collect RPGs?**

- Deep, story-driven experiences that hold up over time
- Often the most valuable games on any platform
- Strong community and appreciation for the genre
- Many titles have never been re-released
- Nostalgia factor and emotional connections run deep

## Genre History

### Origins (1980s)

- **Dragon Quest** (1986) - Defined the JRPG formula
- **Final Fantasy** (1987) - Square's legendary beginning
- **Phantasy Star** (1987) - Sega's sci-fi answer
- **Ultima** series - Western RPG foundations

### Golden Age (1990s)

The SNES and PlayStation eras are considered the pinnacle:

- Square Soft dominated with Final Fantasy IV-IX
- Enix delivered Dragon Quest and action RPGs
- Atlus carved a niche with Shin Megami Tensei
- Working Designs localized treasures for Western audiences

### Modern Evolution

- Many classic series continue today
- Retro-inspired indie RPGs (Undertale, Octopath Traveler)
- Re-releases and remasters have increased accessibility
- Original cartridges/discs remain collectible despite digital options

## Must-Have Games

### Top 10 Definitive RPGs

1. **Chrono Trigger** (SNES) - Perfect pacing, multiple endings, timeless
2. **Final Fantasy VI** (SNES) - Greatest villain, ensemble cast, opera scene
3. **EarthBound** (SNES) - Quirky, heartfelt, unlike anything else
4. **Suikoden II** (PS1) - 108 characters, political intrigue, emotional depth
5. **Final Fantasy VII** (PS1) - Cultural phenomenon, defined an era
6. **Persona 4** (PS2) - Social sim meets dungeon crawler perfection
7. **Dragon Quest VIII** (PS2) - Cel-shaded beauty, classic formula perfected
8. **Xenogears** (PS1) - Ambitious storytelling, deep combat
9. **Phantasy Star IV** (Genesis) - Sega's finest RPG hour
10. **Super Mario RPG** (SNES) - Mario meets Square magic

### Hidden Gems

Underrated titles worth seeking:

- **Lufia II: Rise of the Sinistrals** (SNES) - Puzzle-filled dungeons, Ancient Cave roguelike
- **Terranigma** (SNES PAL) - Soul Blazer trilogy finale, never released in NA
- **Vagrant Story** (PS1) - Dark, complex, underappreciated masterpiece
- **Grandia** (PS1) - Joyful adventure with innovative combat
- **Threads of Fate** (PS1) - Action RPG with dual protagonists
- **Legend of Legaia** (PS1) - Unique martial arts combat system
- **Alundra** (PS1) - Zelda-like with challenging puzzles
- **Radiata Stories** (PS2) - Unique recruitment mechanics, charm
- **Rogue Galaxy** (PS2) - Level-5's space opera adventure
- **Digital Devil Saga** (PS2) - Mature SMT spin-off

### Affordable Entry Points

Great RPGs that won't break the bank:

- **Final Fantasy IV** (SNES) - Classic storytelling, ~$60 CIB
- **Dragon Quest VII** (PS1) - Massive adventure, ~$30 CIB
- **Tales of Symphonia** (GCN) - Excellent action RPG, ~$40 CIB
- **Skies of Arcadia Legends** (GCN) - Airship exploration, ~$80 CIB
- **Paper Mario** (N64) - Charming, accessible, ~$50 CIB
- **Golden Sun** (GBA) - Portable excellence, ~$30 CIB

## Collecting Strategies

### Building a Core Collection

**Priority Order:**

1. Start with affordable classics (FF series, Dragon Quest)
2. Grab common titles before they become uncommon
3. Save for grail titles (EarthBound, Suikoden II)
4. Consider condition vs. budget trade-offs

### Platform Focus

**Best Platforms for RPG Collecting:**

1. **SNES** - Highest concentration of classics, premium prices
2. **PS1** - Largest library, mixed pricing
3. **PS2** - Affordable now, some rising stars
4. **Saturn** - Rare and expensive, import-heavy

### Series Completionism

**Major Series to Complete:**

- Final Fantasy (I-XII+ main entries)
- Dragon Quest (I-VIII retro era)
- Suikoden (I-V)
- Tales (Phantasia through Symphonia era)
- Shin Megami Tensei / Persona
- Star Ocean
- Breath of Fire

## Market Analysis

### Current Trends

- **PS1/PS2 RPGs** rising as millennials build collections
- **SNES RPGs** stabilizing at high levels
- **Working Designs** titles consistently valuable
- **Atlus** RPGs appreciate faster than average
- **Complete series sets** command premiums

### Future Outlook

- Late PS2 era RPGs may be undervalued currently
- Saturn RPGs likely to remain expensive
- Digital re-releases may suppress some prices
- First-print and sealed items will continue appreciating
