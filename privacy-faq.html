Privacy & Data FAQ

How we protect your collection

Dragon's Hoard Atlas is built to be privacy-first. This page explains what stays on-device, when network calls happen, and how you control backups.

← Back to app Ask a question

What stays on your device?

  • Your collection data (statuses, ratings, tags) and personal notes are stored locally.
  • Backlog, wishlist, and trade markers live in your browser's storage—not a cloud account.
  • Offline mode keeps previously viewed data available even without a connection.

When is Supabase used?

Supabase powers optional catalog metadata and authentication. If you don't enable those features, no Supabase calls are made. Even when connected, your personal collection changes stay local and are never written to Supabase tables.

Backups and restores

  • Use Create Backup in Settings to download an encrypted-free JSON copy.
  • Restore any backup locally—no servers or third parties are involved.
  • You can also export or share the JSON if you want an off-site copy.

What leaves your browser?

  • No analytics or tracking pixels are loaded by default.
  • Media assets and platform metadata are fetched from public sources to power search and covers.
  • If you opt in to Supabase, requests include only what's needed for authentication and catalog lookups.

Need more details?
